A Letter to My Daughter

"My dear little one,

By the time you read this, you'll know that your dad traveled the world — from a small town called Chitradurga to 17 countries across 4 continents. But what I want you to know is not where I went, but what I learned.

I learned that every setback is a setup for a comeback. I lost jobs, faced fears, started over in new countries — and each time, life got better.

I learned that home is not a place — it's the people you love. Your grandparents in Tumkur, your aunt in Bangalore, and you — wherever you are, that's my home.

I learned that the world is big, beautiful, and waiting for you. One day, I'll take you to Mont Blanc, to Yosemite, to Lake Tahoe in winter. And you'll understand why your dad couldn't stop exploring.

But most of all, I want you to know: you are the best place I've ever been. ❤️

— Your Dad, the Born Explorer ♐

The Diary of JP

From Chitradurga to Mont Blanc — 17 countries, 7 chapters, one incredible life.
A Sagittarian who turned every setback into a comeback. ♐

"I evolve from average to excellent when aligned. I rise stronger after each low point. I grow most through travel and challenge."

📖 Chapter 1 — Origins (1980–1998)

I was born on December 11, 1980, in Chitradurga, a small historic town in Karnataka, India. A Sagittarian from day one — the explorer was written in the stars. ♐

I grew up in a middle-class family. Quiet, obedient, curious. My childhood was split between Chitradurga and my parents' native places — Bellary (father's side, Hettina Budhihala — our family deity) and Kunabeva (mother's side). I must have visited each over a hundred times. Those dusty roads, temple bells, and summer holidays shaped who I am.

Dharmasthala became my spiritual anchor — Sri Manjunatha Temple. Every visit brought peace. Raichur was another frequent stop — family connections that kept the roots strong.

🎓 Chapter 2 — Education & Discovery (1998–2002)

In 1998, I moved to Tumkur for engineering. I started with Computer Science, struggled with English and teacher fear, then made the decision that changed everything — switched to Telecom Engineering. It was a game changer. I graduated in the top 2 of my class.

College wasn't just studies. It was freedom. Road trips on rickety buses to Mysore, Goa, Gokarna, Mangalore, Coorg, Bandipur, Ooty. My first beach trip to Goa was unforgettable. Coorg's coffee plantations, Ooty's misty hills, Gokarna's hidden paradise. Kemmangundi in the Kudremukh hills became a favourite I'd return to for 17 years.

I also visited Hyderabad and made a spiritual trip to Srisailam — the Jyotirlinga temple. Jog Falls and Agumbe became regular escapes — raw nature at its best.

💼 Chapter 3 — First Steps (2002–2004)

2002 was brutal. The dot-com recession hit. Months of job searching, rejection after rejection. It built a resilience that would carry me through every storm ahead.

Finally, in September 2002, I landed my first job at Velankani Software Systems in Bangalore. Testing role. The door had opened. I was 21, earning my own money, living in India's tech capital. Life had begun.

🌍 Chapter 4 — Wipro & The World Opens (2004–2009)

October 2004 — I joined Wipro Ltd. This would be a decade-long chapter that transformed my life.

Within months, I was on a plane to Espoo, Finland. My first foreign trip. My first onsite. December 2004. I stepped out into -19°C. Snow everywhere. Everything was new, magical, overwhelming. I saw the world differently after that. The quiet kid from Chitradurga was standing in Scandinavia.

During that stay, I took a Viking Lines cruise with friends — Helsinki to Stockholm and back. My first cruise. My first time in Sweden. The Scandinavian winter was harsh but beautiful.

In 2007, I was sent to Suwon, South Korea — Samsung client, long-term onsite. I explored Seoul and Pyeongchang. Back home, I bought a plot and started building a house. Life was accelerating. Transit stops in Singapore and Bangkok added more stamps to the passport.

2008 brought me to Pittsburgh, USA — a 4-month onsite from August to December. I bought my first car in India — a Hyundai i10. From Pittsburgh, I explored the East Coast by car: Niagara Falls (breathtaking), New York, Washington DC, Ohio, Maryland, and the underground wonder of Luray Caverns. Then the 2008 recession hit. The world shook, but the spirit didn't.

🇺🇸 Chapter 5 — The American Dream (2010–2016)

January 2010. H1B visa approved. I moved to San Jose, California. Silicon Valley. The dream was real.

The next six years were the most adventurous of my life. San Francisco became my second home — 20-25 visits. Golden Gate, Fisherman's Wharf, almost every other weekend.

Yosemite National Park became my favourite place on Earth. I went every single year — 2011, 2012, 2013, 2014, 2015, 2016. Half Dome, El Capitan, the waterfalls. Pure magic, every time.

Lake Tahoe was another love — visited in ALL seasons. Snow in winter, crystal blue in summer. 8 visits across 2013-2015. Redwood National Park — walking among the tallest trees on Earth, humbling every time.

The epic 2013 road trip: Yellowstone (Old Faithful, bison, hot springs) → Grand Teton (majestic mountains) → Niagara Falls again → New York. That same year, Dubai for vacation.

2014 was peak America: Grand Canyon (West and South Rim — standing over the abyss on the Skywalk), Death Valley, Las Vegas, San Diego, a cruise from LA to Baja California, Mexico. I became a full-time US employee and bought a Honda CR-V. Freedom earned.

Singapore got a proper visit in 2014. Shirdi twice — 2012 and 2015. Chennai twice. The world was open.

🔄 Chapter 6 — Return & Rebuild (2016–2022)

2016 brought the storm. Layoff. Flight anxiety disorder. I returned to India. It was a low point — but looking back, it was the beginning of the deepest inner work of my life.

Abu Dhabi in 2016. Back to Bangalore. Rebuilding. In 2018, Bangkok and Pattaya — a vacation that reminded me the explorer was still alive. Thailand was vibrant, warm, healing.

2019 — my daughter was born. Life's greatest gift. Everything changed. ❤️ I visited New Delhi and Noida that year. Got a Maruti Brezza Automatic — the family car.

Road trips resumed: Ooty revisited in 2023 (this time in my own car, not a college bus). Wayanad in 2024 — Kerala's green paradise through the Western Ghats. Udupi, Malpe Beach, Blue Lagoon, Murudeshwara — the Karnataka coast in November 2024. Bandipur again in 2024.

🇪🇺 Chapter 7 — Europe (2022–Present)

2022 — a fresh start. I moved to Aachen, Germany. NRW. New country, new language, new chapter. The explorer was back.

By train and bus, I explored: Düsseldorf (10+ times), Cologne (the Dom!), Bonn (Beethoven's city), Dortmund, Wuppertal, Hamburg, Frankfurt. Eifel Rursee became my local escape — 4-6 visits across all seasons. Monschau, the fairy tale village, 3-4 times. Eupen, German-speaking Belgium, right next door.

Across the borders: Maastricht (3-4 times), Liège, Brussels, Antwerp, Ostend, Bruges (medieval fairy tale!), Dinant (saxophone birthplace), Radhadesh (ISKCON temple castle — spiritual peace in Europe). Amsterdam in 2023 — canals, bikes, Van Gogh. Luxembourg twice.

Paris — twice, 2023 and 2024. The Eiffel Tower, the Louvre, the Seine. Magical both times. Switzerland in 2024: Lucerne (Swiss perfection), Rhine Falls (Europe's largest waterfall), Zurich.

The Scandinavia road trip in 2024: drove through Denmark, across the famous Øresund Bridge (undersea!), through Sweden, all the way to Oslo, Norway. Returned by car ferry across the Baltic. Epic.

In October 2025, I got my Toyota Yaris SUV. The European explorer had wheels.

December 2025 — the trip of a lifetime: Strasbourg (Christmas markets), Geneva (Jet d'Eau, Lake Geneva), Chamonix... and then Mont Blanc. 🏔️

Mont Blanc was heaven on Earth. Standing at the roof of Europe, the Alps stretching in every direction, I felt everything — every struggle, every comeback, every mile — had led to this moment. The quiet boy from Chitradurga was standing on top of the world.
